Weight is the measurement of the gravitational force pulling down to the surface. Believe it or not, you do not weigh the same on other planets as you do on Earth! Every planet has different gravitational pull, therefore your weight will change depending on your whereabouts in outerspace. To calculate weight you multiply the mass of an object by the acceleration due to gravity. (9.8 m/s 2 on Earth!).

The law of gravitation is not only applied here on Earth. This formula applies to the whole entire universe. To find the gravity between two objects, the variables you need are both masses, and the distance seperating the center of the objects.

To calculate your weight on different planets, you multiply your mass with the surface gravity of the specified planet. To find your mass, you divide your weight with the surface gravity here on Earth! (9.807 m/s2)
